The Paradise Tanager

The Paradise Tanager, Tangara chilensis, is a medium-sized, brightly colored songbird native to northern South America.

Identification:

The bright light green head and light blue underparts serve as their most distiguishable features. Their upper body plummage is usually black, but subspecies have been identified with the upper body containing red or yellow spots, or a fully red upper body.

Size:

The paradise tanager can vary between 13 cm to 15 cm in length.

Diet:

Their diet consists mainly of fruits, nectar, berries, and insects.
Paradise Tanagers are not considered endangered at this moment.
